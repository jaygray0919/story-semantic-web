Background

Linked Data is a technical format for sharing structured data.

Data often is published on web pages and is readable by humans. However, web page data is difficult to share and cannot be sent easily to another computer for additional processing.

In contrast, Linked Data is readable by computers and is shared & reused across application, enterprise and community boundaries. Sharing & reuse contributes to new information and to automation of activities and processes.

But effective reuse depends on a shared understanding of the meaning of data. For Linked Data, meaning is defined by ontologies. An ontology represents knowledge about a subject in a structured format. The format is a hierarchy of concepts about the subject. An ontology uses a controlled vocabulary to specify types, properties and relationships of concepts.

Lessons learned in managing "big data"

Graham will share experience in dealing with issues such as:

  • Changing a developer's data-orientation from "tables & tuples" to "triples" in the form subject—predicate—object.
  • Managing data sets with 60+ million rows (triples).
  • Using open source tools for analyzing, testing, correcting and deploying Linked Data.
